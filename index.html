Applications

Conservation of Energy

A roller coaster car sits atop a 30-meter tall lift hill. How fast (in m/s) will the car be traveling when it reaches the bottom? (ignore friction and drag)

Solution: Using the conservation of energy, we can set the initial potential energy equal to the final kinetic energy.
$mgh = \frac{1}{2}mv^2$
$v = \sqrt{2gh}$
$v = \sqrt{2 \times 9.8 \times 30} = 24.25 \frac{m}{s}$
